Brand Essence

Who We Are

At Natural de Costa Rica, we believe true coffee excellence begins at origin and is preserved through control, transparency, and respect for nature. We are not intermediaries — we work side by side with producers, overseeing every stage from micro-lot selection to export.

From our farms to your roast,
full control from origin to cup.
Brand Tagline — B2B
Taste Costa Rica,
one micro-lot at a time.
Brand Tagline — B2C
Visual Pillar

Natural

We transmit closeness, nature, and the essence of Costa Rica. Green tones anchor our palette. Imagery reflects the jungle, volcanic highlands, and the authentic origin of our products. Every visual should feel alive, organic, and rooted in the land.

Visual Pillar

Gourmet

We offer a luxury product of unquestionable quality — the customer must perceive this at first glance. Photography is meticulous, high-resolution, and explores diverse perspectives. Layouts are clean and sophisticated. Every detail reinforces value and exclusivity.

Operational Pillar

Traceable

Every batch is traceable from farm to cup. We record altitude, location, variety, process method, and harvest date for each micro-lot. No blending between varieties. Transparency is not a feature — it is the foundation of our credibility.

Operational Pillar

Artisanal

We work in micro-lots of less than 300 kg. Traditional sun drying. Handpicked cherries. Controlled fermentation. We don’t chase volume — we chase flavor, precision, and consistency. Every process is monitored under strict quality control.

Key Brand Facts: 100% Arabica • Poas Volcano, Alajuela, Costa Rica • ±1,500 m.a.s.l. • 3 varieties (Caturra, Sarchimor, Villa Sarchi) • Washed + Red Honey processes • PROCOMER certified • Air-shipped to Europe in 36h

Minimum Clear Space

Maintain a clear zone around the logo equal to the height of the “A” in NATURAL. No text, graphics, or other elements may intrude into this space.

Minimum Size

The logogram alone must not be reproduced smaller than 20px in height on screen or 8mm in print. The full lockup requires a minimum of 100px / 35mm width.

Color Restrictions

The logo must only appear within the established color palette. It may be rendered in brand browns, greens, beige, cream, white, or black — never in unauthorized hues.

Prohibited Uses

Never stretch, rotate, add shadows, apply gradients, change the typeface, alter proportions, or place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without sufficient contrast.

05
Photography

Photography Direction

Our photography must simultaneously feel natural and gourmet. Every image should convey authenticity, quality, and the unique story of Costa Rican volcanic coffee. Careful editing, high resolution, and diverse perspectives are non-negotiable.

Origin & Terroir

  • Volcanic landscapes and jungle canopy
  • Poas Volcano crater and slopes
  • Coffee farms with mountains / drying beds
  • Rich volcanic soil close-ups
  • Sunrise/sunset over highlands

People & Process

  • Producers hand-selecting cherries
  • Workers at the beneficio (Cafe de Altura)
  • Drying beds with parchment coffee
  • Fermentation tanks, washing stations
  • Quality control: cupping, moisture testing

Product & Lifestyle

  • Vacuum-sealed packaging, multiple angles
  • Green beans in detail (texture, color)
  • Roasting machinery (Loring, Probat UG)
  • Pour-over and espresso preparation
  • Gourmet food pairings (pastries, chocolate)

Do

  • Use natural, warm lighting (golden hour preferred)
  • Show real producers and real farms
  • Maintain consistent color grading (warm browns, greens)
  • Include the packaging or logo when possible
  • Explore different perspectives: overhead, macro, wide
  • Ensure every image is high-resolution (min 300 DPI print / 2x screen)

Don’t

  • Use stock photos that feel generic or staged
  • Apply heavy filters or unnatural color grading
  • Show low-quality, blurry, or poorly lit images
  • Use photos that could belong to any coffee brand
  • Mix visual styles (e.g., industrial + rustic)
  • Crop the logo or packaging awkwardly
Photo Captions: When using origin photos, always include a caption that reinforces traceability. Examples: “Micro-lot selection in Costa Rica — every process is traceable” or “Sun drying at 1,500 m.a.s.l., Poas Volcano region.”

07
Tone of Voice

How We Speak

Our voice bridges technical credibility with emotional storytelling. For B2B, data leads and narrative supports. For B2C, narrative leads and data supports. The tone is always confident, warm, precise, and never arrogant.

Confident, Not Arrogant

We state facts with clarity. We let the quality speak. No superlatives without evidence.
Example
“Every batch is traceable, ethically sourced, and handled under our supervision.”

Technical, Yet Accessible

We use precise coffee terminology (SCA, mucilage, fermentation) but always explain the “why” behind the data.
Example
“Fermentation lasts 12 to 72 hours. Perfect timing keeps the cup clean and vibrant — too long, the notes turn sour, and the batch is lost.”

Storytelling with Substance

Every claim is grounded in a verifiable fact: altitude, process, SCA score, origin. We paint a picture, but it’s always real.
Example
“Our coffees are born on the slopes of the Poas Volcano, an active volcano in the province of Alajuela, at altitudes of approximately 1,500 m above sea level.”

Artisanal Precision

We emphasize the human hand and controlled craft. Small scale is a strength, not a limitation.
Example
“We don’t chase volume. We chase flavor, precision, and consistency — so you can taste Costa Rica, one micro-lot at a time.”
B2B vs B2C Priority:
B2B (Traceability Report): Data > Narrative. Lead with metrics, SCA scores, process specifications, certifications, and logistics data. Support with brand story.
B2C (Portfolio, Instagram): Narrative > Data. Lead with sensory descriptions, origin story, and emotional connection. Support with key specs (SCA score, altitude, variety).

Typography in Posts

DM Sans Bold + Uppercase: All cover slides and main text. Ensures consistent grid aesthetic.

Sloop Script Pro: Lowercase only, no bold. Limited to accent phrases (“Pura Vida”, “feel the wild”). Never as primary text.

Corporate Color Accents

Mustard and Terracotta are used exclusively to highlight key data or product characteristics in feed posts. They reinforce the brand’s visual signature and should never dominate the composition — only accent it.

Logo Placement

The NDCR logogram or full lockup must be visible in every post, whether as a watermark, corner element, or integrated into the design. Size and placement adapt to the format while remaining legible.

Content Mix: Alternate between 3 content types per row — (1) Full photo (origin, process, product), (2) Text-over-color (tips, quotes, facts), (3) Product showcase (packaging, beans, cups). This creates visual rhythm and avoids monotony in the grid.
